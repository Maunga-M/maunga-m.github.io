Project 01

Global Sales Performance Analysis (2015 – 2017)

Interactive Tableau dashboard analyzing global revenue trends, product performance, geographic sales distribution, and shipping efficiency to investigate patterns behind a late-period decline in sales activity.

Tableau Data Visualization

Business Problem

A global retail company observed a noticeable decline in revenue and profitability near the end of the reporting period. Leadership wants to understand whether the change reflects shifts in demand, geographic concentration of sales, reliance on specific product categories, or operational factors such as shipping performance.

The analysis focuses on identifying internal performance patterns and potential structural risks within the company's operations.

Key Insights

i. Late-Period Decline Is Driven by Falling Order Volume

Revenue, profit, and order quantity follow nearly identical trajectories over time, signaling stable margins and consistent profitability across the period.

Between September 2017 and October 2017, revenue declined by over 50%. Profit was down by a similar rate while orders decreased by over 80%.

Because these three metrics move together, the decline is more consistent with reduced sales activity than with changes in pricing or cost structure.

ii. Geographic Revenue Is Concentrated but Consistent Across Levels

The top markets remain strong when drilling down into regions and countries, which suggests demand is not driven by a single isolated location but by broader regional strength.

High-performing regions aligned with strong country-level results, indicating that market performance is structurally supported rather than driven by outliers. Lower-performing markets contribute much smaller volumes, which shows that revenue is unevenly distributed geographically.

iii. Product Revenue Is Concentrated in a Few Categories

Fishing generates substantially more revenue than the other categories, which suggests that demand is heavily skewed toward a specific product segment.

The remaining categories contribute meaningful revenue but at noticeably lower levels. This indicates the product portfolio is not evenly balanced and sales rely heavily on a few key products.

iv. Shipping Performance Is Operationally Consistent Across Modes

Average scheduled days range from one to four days, while fulfillment times remain close to five days regardless of shipping method. This suggests the fulfillment process is standardized and does not vary significantly across delivery options.

Profit per shipment is also nearly identical across modes, which indicates shipping speed has little impact on profitability.

Business Implications

  • Sales performance is closely tied to purchasing activity, meaning changes in order volume translate directly into changes in revenue and profit. Because margins appear stable over time, growth will likely depend more on increasing transaction volume than on pricing or cost adjustments.
  • Revenue concentration in a small number of geographic markets increases exposure to regional demand fluctuations. At the same time, strong performance in Latin America and Europe suggests these markets provide a stable foundation for current sales.
  • Dependence on a limited set of product categories creates portfolio risk if demand shifts. However, the strong performance of fishing-related products indicates a segment with clear product-market fit that could support future growth initiatives.
  • Consistent profitability and fulfillment times across shipping modes suggest operational stability in the logistics process. However, the similarity in profit across delivery types indicates that changing shipping speed alone is unlikely to materially improve margins.

Recommendations

  • Increase marketing and sales efforts in lower-revenue regions to diversify revenue sources and reduce reliance on Latin America and Europe.
  • Expand development and promotion of mid-performing product categories to reduce dependency on Fishing while strengthening overall product portfolio balance.
  • Focus growth initiatives on increasing customer demand through targeted marketing campaigns, channel expansion, or strategic partnerships that drive higher order volumes.
  • Conduct additional analysis incorporating external market data, seasonality, or operational changes to determine potential drivers of the decline observed between September 2017 and October 2017.
